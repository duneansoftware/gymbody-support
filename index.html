About GymBody

GymBody is a private, offline-first workout planner and log book for serious lifters and fitness-minded households. It runs entirely on your iPhone — no account, no cloud sync, no analytics, no ads.

  • Adaptive workout planner with Coach, Guide, and Create modes.
  • Live workout logging with quick "kg x reps" entry and on-the-fly exercise swaps.
  • Interactive body map with primary, secondary, and stabilizer roles.
  • Recovery-aware suggestions (Ready / Borderline / Fatigued) based on your last sessions.
  • Activity history browsing by month, list, and exercise.
  • Body measurement tracking with a visual measurement guide.
  • Multiple profiles on one device for households or training partners.
  • Portable .gybd backup and share files.

Frequently asked questions

How do I create my first user profile?

On a fresh install, GymBody opens directly on Settings → Users with a prompt to create your first profile. Tap Add User, enter a name and marker, choose a preferred body model, and save. The app then loads a starter Coach proposal under Next Workout.

How do I plan my next workout?

Open the Next tab. Choose a planning mode:

  • Coach — builds a session from your training history.
  • Guide — tap muscles on the body map; the workout updates as you change selection.
  • Create — start blank and add exercises one at a time.
  • Saved — re-open a previously saved workout.

Use the lock icon to freeze the current draft so refreshes don't overwrite it.

How do I log a workout?

Open Log, then tap Start Workout. Enter sets in the format kg x reps (e.g. 40x10, 50x8, 50x8). Use the swap action on a card if a machine is busy. Tap Finish to save the session into Activity.

How do I back up or share my data?

Go to Settings → Transfer. Export creates a .gybd file containing your selected workout history; you can email it, AirDrop it, or save it via Files. Import brings a .gybd or legacy .json file back in. Planned workouts and history are routed to the right place automatically.

Does GymBody send my data anywhere?

No. GymBody runs entirely on your device. There is no account, sign-in, cloud sync, analytics, or ads. Your training data only leaves the device when you explicitly export a .gybd file.

Why am I asked for HealthKit / location / contacts?

You shouldn't be — GymBody does not request HealthKit, location, contacts, or notification permissions. If iOS prompts you for one of these from another screen while GymBody is in the background, that prompt is not coming from this app.

How do I add an exercise that's not in the catalog?

Open Explore, tap Add manual exercise, and fill in name, primary muscles, and any secondary or stabilizer groups. The new exercise becomes available in Next Workout, Log Workout, and the catalog filters.

How do I reset the intro guide or tab tips?

Go to Settings → Help. Use Play Intro Guide to replay the onboarding, or Show Tab Tips Again to bring back the per-tab help banners.

How do I clear all logged workouts?

Go to Settings → Danger Zone → Clear Workout Activity. This deletes all logged sessions but keeps your saved workouts and user profiles. Export a .gybd backup first if you want to keep a copy.

The app feels stuck or slow. What should I try?
  1. Quit and relaunch GymBody.
  2. Restart your iPhone.
  3. Make sure iOS is up to date (Settings → General → Software Update).
  4. If the issue persists, email us with your device model, iOS version, and a short description.
Will my workouts sync to my other Apple devices?

Not currently. GymBody is intentionally offline. To move data between devices, export a .gybd file from the source device and import it on the target device.

Privacy

GymBody does not collect personal data. Your training history, profiles, and measurements are stored only on your device. The app does not communicate with any server.
